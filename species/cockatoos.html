About Cockatoos

Cockatoos are renowned throughout the avian world for one defining quality above all others: their extraordinary capacity for affection. Often called "velcro birds" by their devoted families, Cockatoos form profoundly close bonds and genuinely thrive on physical closeness, cuddling, and constant companionship.

The Umbrella Cockatoo, with its dramatic pure-white crest and gentle nature, is among the most popular species we work with. The Moluccan, with its blush-pink plumage and heart-melting personality, is perhaps the most beautiful. Goffin's Cockatoos bring extraordinary problem-solving intelligence, while Rose-breasted (Galah) Cockatoos combine affection with playful, entertaining energy.

Owning a Cockatoo is a profound commitment. These birds require — and deserve — enormous amounts of time, attention, and emotional investment. The reward is a relationship of unparalleled warmth.

Caring for a Cockatoo

❤️

Emotional Needs

Cockatoos have exceptionally high social and emotional needs. They require several hours of direct interaction daily and do not cope well with long periods alone. Families who work full-time should seriously consider whether a Cockatoo's needs can be adequately met.

🏠

Housing

A large cage is non-negotiable — minimum 36"×24"×48" for smaller species, considerably larger for Umbrella and Moluccan Cockatoos. A large play stand for out-of-cage time is essential. Cockatoos produce significant feather dust and require good ventilation.

🥦

Nutrition

A pellet-based diet supplemented with fresh vegetables, fruits, and grains provides excellent baseline nutrition. Cockatoos particularly enjoy warm cooked foods like rice, quinoa, and lentils, which also serve as enrichment. Avoid avocado, chocolate, caffeine, and high-fat seeds as staples.

🔊

Vocalisations

Cockatoos can be extremely loud, particularly when they want attention or are startled. Their contact calls are powerful and penetrating. Screaming can develop as a learned behaviour if birds are rewarded with attention during vocalisation — consistent training from an early age is vital.

🧩

Enrichment

Cockatoos need daily enrichment to prevent boredom-related behaviours such as feather plucking. Shredding toys, foraging activities, and training sessions satisfy their active minds. Rotating toys regularly maintains novelty and engagement.

🩺

Health Considerations

Cockatoos are prone to PBFD (Psittacine Beak and Feather Disease), Psittacosis, and feather-destructive behaviour under stress. Annual avian veterinary examinations and a dust-controlled environment are essential to their long-term health.
