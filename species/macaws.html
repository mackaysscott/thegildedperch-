About Macaws

Macaws are the largest members of the parrot family and among the most visually breathtaking creatures on earth. Their vivid plumage — ranging from the electric sapphire and gold of the Blue and Gold Macaw to the blazing crimson of the Scarlet — commands attention in any room they inhabit.

But Macaws are far more than ornamental. They are bold, curious, and deeply affectionate birds that form powerful bonds with their families. A well-socialized Macaw is a constant presence — they want to be involved in everything, and their outgoing personality fills a home with energy and life.

At The Gilded Perch, every Macaw is raised with the care, space, and stimulation these extraordinary birds require — and placed only with families who are truly prepared for the lifelong commitment a Macaw represents.

Ownership Guide

Caring for a Macaw

01

Housing

Macaws require very large enclosures — a minimum of 5 feet wide, 3 feet deep, and 6 feet tall. Many owners supplement with a large play gym. Bar spacing of 1–1.5 inches and stainless steel or powder-coated construction is essential. These birds need space to stretch their impressive wingspan.

02

Nutrition

A Macaw's diet consists of high-quality pellets, fresh fruits, vegetables, and a moderate amount of nuts and seeds. Macaws enjoy foraging, so wrapping food in palm leaves or hiding treats inside foraging toys provides enrichment alongside nutrition.

07

Noise Consideration

Macaws are vocal birds — particularly at dawn and dusk. While their calls are not constant, they are loud when they do vocalise. Prospective owners should honestly assess whether their living situation accommodates a bird whose call can reach 105 decibels.

03

Enrichment

Macaws need 3–4 hours of out-of-cage time and direct interaction daily. Their powerful beaks require tough wooden toys, stainless steel puzzle feeders, and rope toys to satisfy their natural destructive instincts. A bored Macaw can be a destructive Macaw.

08

Handling

Macaws can be extremely affectionate but also possess powerful beaks capable of significant damage if frightened or provoked. Positive reinforcement training from an early age is essential. Well-trained Macaws are extraordinarily gentle, loyal companions.

06

Family Suitability

Macaws thrive in active, engaged households. They love activity and are well-suited to families who can provide consistent daily interaction. They require experienced owners who understand positive reinforcement and can commit to the lifetime their bird may live.
