Psittacus erithacus

About the African Grey

The African Grey is widely considered the most intelligent bird species on the planet. Originating in the equatorial rainforests of West and Central Africa, these remarkable birds have been documented demonstrating cognitive abilities on par with five-year-old children — including object permanence, basic arithmetic, and nuanced emotional understanding.

Dr. Irene Pepperberg's decades of research with the famous African Grey Alex demonstrated that these birds are not merely mimics — they comprehend the meaning of words, understand categories, and can reason about the world around them. Living with an African Grey is a privilege unlike any other in the avian world.

At The Gilded Perch, our African Greys are hand-raised from hatch, exposed to rich environmental stimulation, and paired with dedicated families who understand the depth of commitment this extraordinary species requires.

Ownership Guide

Caring for an African Grey

🏠

Housing

African Greys require a minimum cage size of 36"×24"×48" — larger is always better. Bar spacing should be no greater than 1 inch. Stainless steel construction is ideal. The cage should be placed in a social area of the home but away from draughts and direct sunlight.

🥦

Nutrition

A balanced diet consists of a high-quality formulated pellet base (60–70%), supplemented with fresh vegetables, leafy greens, fruits, and limited seeds. African Greys are particularly prone to calcium and Vitamin A deficiency — a varied, nutrient-dense diet is critical to their longevity.

🧩

Mental Stimulation

African Greys require 3–4 hours of direct social interaction and mental enrichment daily. Foraging toys, puzzle feeders, training sessions, and free flight time out of the cage are essential. A bored African Grey may develop feather-destructive behaviours — mental engagement is non-negotiable.

❤️

Emotional Wellbeing

African Greys are sensitive, emotionally perceptive birds that form deep attachments. They can sense tension in the household and may react to stressful environments. Consistency, gentle handling, and a calm daily routine are the foundations of a content African Grey.

🩺

Veterinary Care

Annual wellness examinations by a certified avian veterinarian are essential. African Greys are prone to Psittacine Beak and Feather Disease (PBFD), Aspergillosis, and calcium-related metabolic disorders. Early detection is key to long-term health.

👨‍👩‍👧

Family Suitability

African Greys generally bond most deeply with one or two primary caregivers, though well-socialized birds adjust well to family environments. They are not ideal for households with very young children due to their sensitivity to unpredictable movement and noise.
